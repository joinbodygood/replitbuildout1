Integrate PayPal Subscriptions for all products that have a recurring/subscription component. This needs to be built out before we migrate anything from Shopify.

1. PayPal Subscription Setup

Integrate PayPal's Subscription API to handle all recurring payments. Every product that offers a multi-month plan (3-month, 6-month) or ongoing monthly subscription should use PayPal's subscription billing — not one-time payments.

2. Products that need subscription billing

Map out and connect PayPal subscriptions for every product that has a recurring component. This includes:

Weight Loss (Compounded GLP-1):

Monthly plan: $169/mo recurring
3-month plan: $149/mo recurring (billed monthly for 3 months)
6-month plan: $139/mo recurring (billed monthly for 6 months)
Wellness Injections:

Any product offered on a monthly or multi-month recurring basis
Hair Loss:

Any product offered on a monthly or multi-month recurring basis
Feminine Health:

Any product offered on a monthly or multi-month recurring basis
For each subscription product, create a corresponding PayPal Subscription Plan with:

The correct billing cycle (monthly)
The correct number of cycles (1 for monthly, 3 for 3-month, 6 for 6-month — or ongoing if it auto-renews)
The correct price per cycle
3. One-time payment products (NOT subscriptions)

These should remain as standard PayPal one-time payments, NOT subscriptions:

Brand Rx consultation fee ($55) — one-time
Mental Health consultation fee ($25) — one-time
Ondansetron upsell ($10) — one-time
Any other single-purchase items
4. Checkout integration

Update the checkout page to use PayPal's subscription buttons/flow for subscription products:

When the cart contains a subscription product, render the PayPal Subscribe button (not the standard Pay button)
Show the billing terms clearly before the customer confirms: "You will be billed $149/month for 3 months" or "$169/month (cancel anytime)" depending on the plan
After successful PayPal subscription creation, store the PayPal Subscription ID in the order record and the patient's account
Handle subscription webhooks from PayPal: payment success, payment failed, subscription cancelled, subscription expired
5. Subscription management

In the admin dashboard:

Show each patient's active subscriptions with PayPal Subscription ID, status, next billing date, and amount
Allow admins to view, pause, or cancel subscriptions
In the patient portal:

Show the patient their active subscription details
Allow patients to cancel their subscription (with a confirmation step)
6. Mixed carts (subscription + one-time)

If the cart has both a subscription item (e.g., 3-month GLP-1 plan) and a one-time item (e.g., Ondansetron upsell):

Process the subscription through PayPal Subscriptions
Process the one-time item as a separate PayPal one-time payment, OR add it as a setup fee on the subscription (first payment = subscription price + one-time item)
Clearly show both charges in the checkout summary
Build this subscription infrastructure first. Once PayPal subscriptions are working and tested, we will proceed with the Shopify migration.

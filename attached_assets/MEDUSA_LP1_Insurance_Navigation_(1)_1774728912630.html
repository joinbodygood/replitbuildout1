Quiz Landing Block

What Are Your Chances of Getting Approved?

Not all insurance plans are created equal. Your approval odds depend on your carrier, your plan type, your diagnosis, and which medication you want. Our free probability quiz uses real coverage data to give you an honest estimate — before you spend a dime.

Quiz Questions (5-6 questions, under 60 seconds):

Q1: Who is your insurance carrier?
Options: UnitedHealthcare, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, Aetna, Humana, Kaiser, TRICARE, Medicaid, Medicare, Other
Routing: Each carrier has a base probability score. TRICARE = high. Medicare = flag for limitations.

Q2: What type of plan do you have?
Options: Through my employer, Private/marketplace, Medicaid, Medicare, Military/TRICARE, Not sure
Routing: Employer plans with obesity rider = highest probability. Medicaid = state-dependent. Medicare = weight loss not covered (flag).

Q3: Have you been diagnosed with any of these conditions?
Options (multi-select): Type 2 diabetes, Prediabetes/insulin resistance, High blood pressure, High cholesterol, Sleep apnea, Heart disease, PCOS, None of these
Routing: T2D = highest approval odds (Mounjaro/Ozempic indication). Sleep apnea = Zepbound pathway. Heart disease = Wegovy CV indication. More conditions = higher probability.

Q4: Which medication are you most interested in?
Options: Wegovy (semaglutide for weight loss), Zepbound (tirzepatide for weight loss), Mounjaro (tirzepatide for diabetes), Ozempic (semaglutide for diabetes), I'm not sure — help me choose
Routing: Diabetes medications (Mounjaro/Ozempic) generally have higher approval rates than weight-loss-specific medications (Wegovy/Zepbound).

Q5: Have you tried diet and exercise for weight loss in the past?
Options: Yes, multiple times, Yes but with limited success, I'm currently trying, No
Routing: Prior failed lifestyle modification strengthens PA arguments. "Yes, multiple times" = highest weight.

Q6 (optional): What is your approximate BMI?
Options: Under 27, 27-29, 30-34, 35-39, 40+, I'm not sure
Routing: BMI 30+ = meets obesity threshold. BMI 27-29 + comorbidity = meets overweight threshold. Under 27 = may not qualify.

FAQ
Accordion — 10 Questions
What is the free probability quiz?
It's a quick 60-second quiz that estimates how likely your insurance is to cover GLP-1 weight loss medication. It uses your carrier, plan type, and health conditions to give you an honest probability score. No insurance card needed. No cost. No commitment.
What does the $25 eligibility check include?
We use your actual insurance card and member ID to run a real coverage verification. You'll get a detailed report showing: whether your plan covers GLP-1 medications, which specific medications are covered, your estimated copay, and whether a prior authorization is required. Results within 48 hours.
What is a prior authorization and why do I need one?
A prior authorization (PA) is your insurance company's way of confirming that a medication is medically necessary before they agree to pay for it. Many insurance plans require a PA for GLP-1 medications. Our clinical team writes and submits your PA, including all the clinical documentation your insurance needs to say "yes."
What happens if my prior authorization is denied?
If your PA is initially denied, our team files one appeal on your behalf at no additional cost (it's included in the $50 PA submission fee). Many denials are overturned on appeal. If the appeal is also denied, we'll help you explore self-pay options starting at $149/month so you're never left without a path forward.
How much will I pay at the pharmacy?
If your insurance covers your medication and you qualify for the manufacturer savings card, most patients pay around $25/month at the pharmacy. Your exact cost depends on your plan's copay structure, which we'll confirm during the eligibility check.
Which medications can you help me get covered?
We work with all four major GLP-1 medications: Wegovy (semaglutide, for weight loss — now available as a pill or injection), Zepbound (tirzepatide, for weight loss and sleep apnea), Mounjaro (tirzepatide, for type 2 diabetes), and Ozempic (semaglutide, for type 2 diabetes). We'll recommend the one that gives you the best chance of approval based on your insurance and health history.
Do I have to commit to the $75/month program?
No. The $75/month ongoing program is pay-as-you-go with no contract. You can cancel anytime. It includes monthly prescription renewals, unlimited clinical team support, PA renewal handling, and dosage adjustments. Think of it as having a concierge medical team for your weight loss — without the concierge price tag.
I have Medicare or Medicaid. Can I still use this program?
It depends. Medicare currently does not cover medications prescribed specifically for weight loss. However, if you have type 2 diabetes, Medicare may cover Ozempic or Mounjaro for blood sugar management. Medicaid coverage varies by state. Our $25 eligibility check will give you a definitive answer for your specific situation. If insurance doesn't work out, our self-pay programs are available to everyone.
Is Body Good a real medical practice?
Yes. Body Good Studio is a physician-led telehealth practice founded by Dr. Linda Moleon, who is double board-certified in Obesity Medicine and Anesthesiology. Every prescription is reviewed by our licensed medical team. We are not a pharmacy, a supplement company, or a wellness brand. We are a legitimate medical practice that specializes in weight management.
What if my insurance doesn't cover it?
You still have excellent options. Our self-pay compounded programs start at $149/month for semaglutide and $315/month for tirzepatide, with free shipping and full physician oversight. We also offer oral (needle-free) options and branded prescription management starting at $45/month. The $25 eligibility check is never wasted — it gives you clarity either way.

A. Quiz Overhaul

The current feminine health quiz is too generic and doesn't collect enough information to make a targeted recommendation. Expand it substantially.

Questions:

Primary concern — What brings you here today? (Single select)

Vaginal dryness or discomfort
Recurrent yeast infections
Bacterial vaginosis (BV)
UTI prevention
Low libido or decreased sexual desire
Hormonal changes (perimenopause, menopause, irregular periods)
General intimate wellness / not sure — help me figure it out
Symptom duration — How long have you been experiencing this? (Less than 1 month, 1-3 months, 3-6 months, 6+ months, On and off for years)

Severity — How much is this affecting your daily life or relationships? (Mildly — it's manageable, Moderately — it's becoming a problem, Significantly — it's affecting my quality of life)

Previous treatments — Have you tried anything for this before? Select all that apply. (Over-the-counter products, Prescription medication, Hormone therapy, Home remedies, Nothing — this is my first time seeking help)

Current medications — Are you currently taking any medications, including birth control or hormone therapy? (Yes — with text field, No)

Life stage — Which best describes where you are right now? (Pre-menopausal / regular cycles, Perimenopause — cycles becoming irregular, Menopause — no period for 12+ months, Post-menopause, Prefer not to say)

Contraindications / safety screen — Do any of the following apply to you?

Currently pregnant or breastfeeding
History of hormone-sensitive cancer (breast, ovarian, uterine)
Currently experiencing unexplained vaginal bleeding
If any are selected: show a compassionate message redirecting to a consultation. "For your safety, we recommend speaking directly with our physician team before proceeding." CTA: "Schedule a Consultation"
Treatment preference — Do you have a preference for how you'd like to use your treatment? (Topical cream or gel, Oral medication, No preference — whatever works best)

Quiz tone guidelines:

Use warm, normalizing language throughout. Add reassuring copy like "You're not alone — many women experience this" and "There are no wrong answers"
Avoid clinical jargon where possible — use plain language
Add a brief intro screen: "This quiz helps our physician team understand your intimate health needs so they can recommend the right solution. Your answers are completely confidential."
